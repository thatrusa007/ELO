Classification of Living Organisms

Explore the Five Kingdoms of Life

Monera Protista Fungi Plantae Animalia

Kingdom Monera

Monera includes prokaryotic organisms such as bacteria. They are unicellular and lack a nucleus.

Kingdom Protista

Protists are mostly unicellular eukaryotic organisms. Examples include Amoeba and Paramecium.

Kingdom Fungi

Fungi are multicellular (except yeasts) organisms that absorb nutrients from organic material.

Kingdom Plantae

Plants are multicellular, photosynthetic organisms. They form the base of most ecosystems.

Kingdom Animalia

Animals are multicellular, heterotrophic organisms with complex structure and function.
